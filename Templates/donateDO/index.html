OUR NEXT STORY

"We're turning the page toward a steadfast future."

The Daily Orange has served the Syracuse community for 116 years. Your donation helps The D.O. keep doing what it does best.

Every dollar supports our mission of producing top-notch local journalism and creating opportunities for students.

For 116 years, The D.O. has covered Syracuse like no other while training generations of journalists, innovators and leaders.

As The D.O. moves down the block, to 230 Euclid Ave., one era ends. But another is just beginning. We're launching a rigorous fundraising effort to ensure the future of one of Syracuse's most integral and venerated entities. The D.O. is a nonprofit, independent, student-run paper. We don't receive a cent from Syracuse University. That's by choice; Our editorial independence means that much to us.

Like newsrooms around the country, we’ve felt the effects of an industry in peril. Since 2013, our advertising revenue has dropped by 45 percent. The D.O.'s leadership has cut expenses by 35 percent in the past six years, including reducing print frequency to three days a week. We're running out of ways to save money.

Even the face of painstaking decisions and cutbacks, we've maintained our status as the best collegiate newspaper in the country. We need your help more than ever.

If The D.O. wants to continue producing journalism at an award-winning level, there's little left to cut. If we want to continue to develop the next generation of groundbreaking communicators, we must turn the tide.

In our 117th year, help us write our next story.
